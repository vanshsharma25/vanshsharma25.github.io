About Me

I am an Automotive engineer specialising in vehicle dynamics and simulation, currently completing an MSc in Motorsport Engineering at Oxford Brookes University with a strong trajectory toward distinction.

My academic background - a BEng in Automobile Engineering followed by postgraduate research - has centred on how vehicles behave under dynamic loading. My MSc thesis explores the application of tuned mass dampers and inertial dampers in off-road motorcycles, a project that highlights my ability to combine theory, simulation, and practical design to solve vibration and fatigue challenges.

Skilled in Multibody Dynamics (MSC Adams), CFD (STAR-CCM+), and FEA (FEMAP), I bring both technical expertise and a strong interest in applying engineering principles to real-world motorsport and automotive problems.

While I enjoy working across all vehicle platforms, I have a particular passion for motorcycle performance and rider safety - balancing agility, comfort, and durability. My goal is to contribute to teams pushing the boundaries of vehicle dynamics, simulation, and safety innovation in motorsport and performance engineering.
