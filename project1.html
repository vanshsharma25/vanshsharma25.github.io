Potential Benefits of Tuned Mass Dampers and Inerters in Offroad Two-Wheelers

Objective

Off-road bicycles and motorcycles encounter extreme suspension demands, ranging from low-displacement, high-frequency vibrations on rough trails to high-displacement, low-frequency impacts during jumps and obstacles. Conventional hydraulic dampers face inherent trade-offs between managing these regimes, leading to compromised ride comfort and reduced control. This study investigates the potential benefits of tuned mass dampers (TMDs) and inertial dampers (inerters) as complementary suspension devices to enhance damping performance across a broad frequency spectrum.

Tools Used

  • MSC Adams
  • MATLAB
  • SOLIDWORKS
  • Python and VS Code

Methodology

The methodology integrates theoretical modelling, CAD-based feasibility studies, and dynamic simulation. It is designed to systematically evaluate the performance of TMDs and inerters against hydraulic dampers under realistic off-road conditions, with reproducibility and scalability as key considerations.

Results

Results indicate that mass-based damping devices can effectively attenuate high-frequency as well as mid-frequency inputs while maintaining ground contact, with reduced complexity compared to hydraulic systems. Although the study is primarily simulation-driven, the findings suggest a promising, scalable pathway for enhancing off-road two-wheeler suspension performance.

  • TMD integration into automotive shock absorbers could achieve up to 42% vibration reduction without requiring fundamental redesign.
  • Improved ride stability by approximately 12%
